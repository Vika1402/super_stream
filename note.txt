Cookies are used to manage sessions in web applications, helping the website remember users as they browse. For example, when you log into a website, a cookie is created to store a session ID or token. This cookie is sent back to the website with every request, so the server knows who you are and keeps you logged in. Itâ€™s also used to track things like items in your shopping cart.
